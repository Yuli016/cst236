The Legend of Zelda Gameplay Innovations


Controls

D-Pad: Moves Link left and right, up and down as this is a top-view game. Also moves the inventory cursor.
A Button: Swings your sword when when you push this button. Also used to confirm a selection.
B Button: Pushing this button will make you use whichever item is currently equipped. You can equip an item to the B button by selecting it in the inventory.
Select: Selects options and also pauses the game.
Start: Also able to pause the game as well as bringing up the subscreen.


Overworld

Most other games would focus on getting high scores but in The Legend of Zelda game during it’s time it created a new kind of gameplay and allowed players to be able to save their progress to later resume playing where they left off. The gameplay is mainly just exploring the overworld in a top view perspective fighting monsters, collecting rupees to buy useful items and upgrading weapons in shops, finding secret location to collect items and finding all 8 locations of the dungeons (The Eagle, The Moon, The Manji, The Snake, The Lizard, The Dragon, The Demon, The Lion) to beat. Because the game is nonlinear it allows players to freely go where they want in the overworld and complete the dungeons in any order because you need to collect all 8 pieces of the Triforce in order to enter Death Mountain to defeat Ganon.




























When you enter a dungeon it’s a labyrinth with many rooms filled with monsters and useful items to collect but sometimes you’re unable to beat a dungeon if they don’t have a specific item to continue going forward. Some rooms you can avoid fighting enemies and go to the next room but sometimes you have to kill all the enemies to collect a small key to open a door. Each dungeon provides a compass to help show the player the location of the piece of Triforce, a dungeon map showing the layout of the rooms of how it’s connected, and a new item to use. When you beat the boss of the dungeon they drop a heart container optioninal for the player to collect to increase their health. Once you collect one of the pieces of the Triforce you exit the dungeon and appear in front of the entrance and continue to find the other dungeons.

Second Quest is unlocked when you beat the game or when you enter the name “ZELDA” as the player’s name. During the Second Quest the game is more difficult for players wanting a more challenging experience. The difference is the location of the Dungeons and items are located in different areas making the player be able to re-experience exploring the overworld to find them while fighting even stronger enemies.

Overworld



Because Ocarina of Time is the first game in the series to use 3D graphics Nintendo included a new mechanic Z-targeting when pressing the Z trigger it locks onto an enemy or object and the camera follows the target to make Link face it. When there is no target to lock on when pressing the Z the camera would just lock Link into facing the direction he’s in to help have a reliable control of the camera in 3D space. Z-Targeting is an important innovation that is used in many 3D combat games.


Controllers are limited to the amount of buttons they have and Nintendo used Context-Sensitive to allow one button to have many different actions depending on Link’s surroundings to interact with the environment changing the A button actions to understand the player’s intent. The A button can be used to climb a box when Link is near the box he can climb on top of it and if Link is near a door can open the door.



The player is taught songs by various characters to be able to use to solve puzzles, call Epona to ride across Hyrule, change the time in Hyrule to day or night, cause rain, and after beating a temple be able to wrap there by playing the song in the correct order key button on the ocarina.



The concept of time travel allows players to explore Hyrule into two different eras of the present and the future. In the present Link is a child and Hyrule has some dangers and has to collect 3 Spiritual Stones to open the door to the Sacred Realm. Young Link can’t change tunics, boots, or swords because they are adult size, or use any weapons that adult Link can use but can crawl into small areas. In the future Link is an adult and Hyrule is plagued by curses and has to free five temples from Ganondorf’s control and awaken each temple sage to help seal Ganondorf in the Dark Realm. Adult Link can change his tunics to survive high temperatures, and breathe underwater, use iron boots to walk underwater, and use hover boots to walk on air for a short time. Only adult Link can ride Epona across Hyrule because Epona is old enough to carry Link. Changing between the present and future is caused by putting or taking the Master Sword. Most of the change of switching is mainly the weapons and equipment Link can use. The overworld is the same, just the look is different.

Overworld


Nintendo always tries to do something unique for The Legend of Zelda games and for Skyward Sword it heavily uses Motion Controls to allow players to experience wielding a sword to fight enemies. Skyward Swords require players to use a Wii Remote Plus that has a built in motion control or a Wii Motion Plus attached to your Wii Remote while also using a nunchuck attached to the Wii Remote. The Wii Remote serves as Link’s sword and the Nunchuck as the shield during your adventures. Raising the Nunchuk causes Link to raise his shield up in order to block enemy’s attacks and to reflect enemy’s projectiles to them. Because the shield can be damaged from enemy attack, players have to think of more strategic ways to defend against the enemy than relying too much on the shield to protect Link because if it breaks you’ll have to buy a new one or repair it. Skyward Strike is caused when the player raises the Wii Remote up in the air to cause the sword to charge up and when it’s finished charging, swinging the Wii Remote down to release a beam as a projectile attack and can trigger environmental elements like magical switches.


A new function was introduced, the stamina gauge allowing Link to be able to sprint and dash up walls, and use for sword attacks but the meter decreased quickly, limiting Link’s movement instead of him being able to perform these actions for an infinite amount of time. If the meter is drained, Link becomes exhausted for a while, unable to use any weapons or items until the gauge is refilled and during battles it leaves Link defenseless. When stamina isn’t used it will slowly refill and completely refill when Link collects a Stamina Fruit.


The sailcloth allows Link to safely land on the ground to avoid fall damage. The sailcloth only goes straight down and doesn’t allow the player to steer in different directions. When there is a geyser or windy gust Link can use the sailcloth to ride the winds to reach higher areas.


The inventory is assessed by pressing and holding the B button to bring up the menu and using the Wii Remote to just point or tilt the direction of the item you want selected, allowing players to use the item in real time.


There are some people who enjoy the motion control to swing a sword to play but some people don't like that Skyward Sword is heavily motion controlled. Nintendo is going to re-release Skyward Swords HD for the Switch on July 16, 2021. Allowing players to choose their experience in how to play by using a controller to press buttons or to use joycons for motion control to still experience swinging a sword like in the Wii.

Overworld



Breath of the Wild is the first open world Zelda game. An open world presents many new gameplay opportunities. In order to make traveling the open world danamic and interesting, Breath of the Wild implemented many new gameplay features.

Breath of the Wild uses a rune system to give the player more tools for exploring, combat and puzzle solving. The Stasis rune allows the player to freeze a moving object or a monster for a short period of time. While the object or monster is frozen the player can use their weapon to hit the object or monster causing it to gain kinetic energy. Once time runs out the object or monster will be affected by the stored kinetic energy. The strength and direction are shown by an arrow. This rune is useful in puzzles and combat.


The Magnesis rune acts like a giant magnet for the player to use. It can freely lift any metal object from weapons to large metal boxes. By observing their surroundings the player can use this rune to aid in combat and puzzle solving.


The Cryonis rune can be used on a body of water to create a block of ice taller than Link. These pillars can be climbed for traversing when exploring. Also the pillars can be used as obstacles when in combat.


Breath of the Wild added a variety of weapons to the game. The player can use over 120 unique weapons found throughout the map, with the weapons fitting into 9 different categories with bows and shields as other options. The large selections of weapons come with a new system, weapon durability. Weapons will break over time after extended use in puzzle, combat, or on impact after being thrown at an enemy. While the player can only hold a limited number of weapons there are weapons scattered all across the map encouraging the player to explore.
